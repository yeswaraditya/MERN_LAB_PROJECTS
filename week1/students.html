Students Corner

Welcome to the Students Corner! This section provides information and resources for current students of PVP Siddhartha Institute of Technology.

Anti Ragging Committee

The Anti Ragging Committee is formed as per UGC Regulations and AP Prohibition Act, 1997 to ensure a safe and healthy environment for all students. Below are the key components:

  • UGC Regulations
  • AP Prohibition Act, 1997
  • Committee
  • Squad
  • Booklet
  • Clauses as per UGC regulations
  • UGC Annexure I
  • UGC Annexure II

Anti-Ragging Committee Duties

The Anti-Ragging Committee has the following duties:

  • Committee formed with senior faculty, led by the Principal, and includes residents, parents, and leaders from surrounding areas.
  • Steps have been taken to ensure faculty presence in all key areas (canteens, parking lots, blocks, playgrounds, etc.) to prevent ragging activities.
  • Awareness campaigns via Flexes, Posters, and Boards in and around college premises to discourage ragging.
  • The Grievances & Redressel committee is part of anti-ragging activities.
  • Awareness programs such as meetings and PPT sessions for senior students.
  • The Anti-Ragging Squad conducts surprise raids in hostels and other vulnerable areas, empowered to inspect and address ragging incidents.
  • Each member is responsible for maintaining an activity sheet for their duties, highlighting steps taken to prevent ragging.

National Service Scheme (NSS)

The NSS motto, "Not Me But You," reflects the essence of democratic living and emphasizes selfless service. Here are some highlights:

  • Understanding the community, identifying problems, and engaging in community problem-solving.
  • Developing social and civic responsibility and utilizing knowledge for practical solutions.
  • Activities like 'National Integration', 'Clean and Green', 'Blood donation', 'Medical camps', 'Village Adoption', 'Environmental awareness', and more.
  • Voluntary blood donation, adopted villages, literacy campaigns, energy conservation, and cleanliness drives.

National Cadet Corps (NCC)

The NCC Unit, established in 1982, focuses on developing character, discipline, leadership, and national service. The cadets participate in various social and community programs:

  • Flood Relief Camps, Tree Plantation, Blood donation, and awareness programs on social issues.
  • Annual Training Camps, National Integration Camps, and other significant events like Republic Day and Independence Day camps.
  • Adventure Training, First Aid, Weapon Training, Disaster Management, and more.
  • Students who participate are awarded NCC A, B, and C Certificates for their contributions.
